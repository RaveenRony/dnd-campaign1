*"The Land of Kings"*
*Monarchy, Arcane Manipulation, and Courtly Intrigue*

Azeroth is the proud seat of the human empire, known for its knightly orders, flourishing trade, and grand cities. But after the Shattering, a sense of decay and forgotten sin lingers in its corners. Being mortal compared to their allies, the humans had always kept caution against other species until the current king **Arthur Grant II** who pushed for the formation of [[The Triumvirate]]. 

Azeroth is heart of the trading between The Triumvirate with several outposts of the other islands in its major city **Greenhaven**. The major trade from Greenhaven being fish and crops procured from **Pinewood**, which is a village that is surrounded by a thick forest that has been thinning over the ages to support the growth of the human empire. The main deity of these lands being Boldrei for Pinewood and Kol Korran in Greenhaven. 

<h4>Greenhaven</h> 
The capital of Azeroth and the seat of human power. Walled and divided into noble districts, trade quarters, and arcane enclaves. A beacon of law and pride, now under pressure as instability rises from their heirless king aging. The Wizard Tower of Elaria being maintained nearby has started to cause an influx of powerful magic users and a surge of offspring that are blessed with the power of mana. The Wizard Tower acts as a hub connecting the isles for ease of travel and communication. Current head of the Wizard Tower being a human Wizard named **Zarkon Sonier**.

Major Locations
 - Palace - North of the City - King Arthur Grant II and his council of ministers are the main residents. Guarded by the Order of Knights, that comprises of powerful fighters and paladins trained by the strongest in the The Triumvirate.
 - Barracks - East of the Palace - Training area and living quarters for the guards that aren't posted on the outskirts in anticipation of attacks.
 - Crimson Monastery - South of the City - Safe haven and training grounds for monks and mortals that wish to understand the monastic ways of life. The monks has entered an agreement of non-aggression and alliance with Azeroth. Hosts a tournament every year to find the **Master of the Open Hand**.
 - Trader Outpost - Center of the City - Bustling warehouses with goods from [[Ortan Thaig]] and [[Seluris]] passing through here. Known for its open market with rare magical items and being heavily guarded by the city guards.
 - Thieves Guild - West of the City - Disguised as unassuming tailor's shop, it is the hidden nerve center of all illicit activities of Azeroth. A secret benefactor is rumoured to run the operation, only known as Mistress Vale. Specialising in smuggling, forgery and high-profile heists, which are done through recruiting unassuming adventurers.
 - Adventurers Guild - West of the City - Serves as a gathering place for adventurers, mercenaries and glory-seekers. Offers contracts, missions and bounties ranging from low-paying to high-paying jobs.
 - Church of Aureon - Center of the City - Grand domed temple dedicated to Aureon. It mainly acts as a place of worship and legal archive to the common people but to the unruly, the church is known to act as a small smuggling ring that distribute illegal items.

<h4>Pinewood</h>
A small forest-bordering village nestled near the southern coastline. Once a logging and hunting town, now has turned into the main hub of crops, fish and timber for the main city, Greenhaven. Governed by Mayor Eliza that was elected by King Arthur Grant II to maintain a good relationship with the commoners. Misty, quiet, wary. Locals speak of “ghost winds” and refuse to enter the forest at night with rumours often spread by Old Man Jeff. Pinewood was plagued by vanishing animals, strange lights in the forest, and whispers of old things waking in the deep woods.

Major Locations
 - Farms - North-East of the village - Acres of land owned by Greg and Baelin, who work in keeping up the supply for the village and city.
 - Hollow Glen - North of the village - Surrounds the village with thick large pine trees that seem unusual to appear in a temperate area.
 - Mayor's Hall - Center of the village - Working quarters of the resolute Mayor Eliza, who seem to be the glue holding the village together.
