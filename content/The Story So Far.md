Ever since, he woke up with a gap of 7 years in his memories, Astor Mephisto has been  looking for odd jobs and bounties to get some money to find ways to get back the memories of 7 years he had lost. Living in GreenHaven, Astor went to the bar that he usually frequents to collect bounties. Walking over someone who had been kicked out of the bar, Astor entered the bar to find a quest asking for help in Pinewood, a village past the strange forest he had come out of when he woke up in the blaze. Seeing that, he starts to make his way towards the village, having to cross the forest that he had once ran out off after waking up when he lost all his memories.

Bhardrus had trained with the Dwarven army ever since he grew up in the island 10 years ago. He was given the position of support staff even though he was training to be a soldier and a paladin. So when he was deployed to Cragmore, he thought he would get more time to train and rise up the ranks but fortunately or unfortunately, Commander Arden sent him to Azeroth, a island that was predominantly ruled by humans. Due to Bhardrus's human nature, the commander thought it would be best to send him to the island in response to the request of help to handle the strange phenomena. Bhardrus had been teleported by an old human and was sent to the magic tower in Azeroth, from where he made his way to the village Pinewood.

Jozeth Joestar had been travelling in hiding throughout Seluris and living in the outskirts of GreenHaven, being trained by his mentor Gyro Zeppeli. Soon after giving him all the theoretical knowledge that he needed to proceed, Zeppeli reminded him about his goals in life and left him to gain experience in the real world. He found out later that he was robbed by Zeppeli, to force him to start from nothing, which was when he was kicked out of a bar for drinking without paying. Before leaving though, he had caught rumours on some strange issues happening in Pinewood, and started to head that way to see if he could get some money. 

Varis Siannodel had left the Avloria Grove in search of a cure for the decay of the Grove. He went around the world to research the life pulsing from the trees. His first stop was the lush forests of Myrraline, but something or someone had sent him back to the forests of Azeroth. This was where he met a squirrel that offered him information but he lied to the squirrel and only got half the information before attacking the squirrel rushed off, implying that it was something powerful in disguise.

The four PCs find themselves in Pinewood, gathered in the mayor Eliza's office, where they introduce themselves to one another while receiving information from the mayor about the strange events occurring in the village. Since their objectives were all the same, they decided to proceed with the quest together and split the money equally. Seeking further details, they move through Pinewood and speak with villagers who appear to have relevant knowledge. After collecting enough information, they venture into the nearby forest and discover an ancient ruin previously mentioned by Old Man Jeff.

Upon entering the ruin, the party descends into its basement after beating up some goblins that were guarding the entrance, where they encounter a humanoid performing a ritual sacrifice. Blood from the victims flows into a mirror that remains unnaturally clean despite the constant stream. The cultist reveals himself to be a member of the Cult of the Mindweaver and attempts to flee, but he is ultimately defeated by the party. The PCs rescue the remaining missing villagers and recover the mirror, revealed to be a powerful artifact.

While resting within the ruins, the party encounters a gelatinous cube that initially shows no hostility. After provoking the creature, it becomes aggressive and attacks. Unwilling to draw attention to their actions, the PCs flee the area, hoping to conceal their involvement in the creature’s death. Unbeknownst to them, the gelatinous cube was the pet of an Archfey named Silvertail.

Upon returning to the mayor Eliza, the party is instructed to seek an audience with the king, who can explain the necessity of locating the remaining artifacts that were rumoured to be of the same set as the mirror that they hold. After a day of rest and celebration, they depart Pinewood and travel toward Greenhaven. During their journey, the party discovers that they have become soul-linked through the Mirror. Any attempt by one of them to leave the group results in intense psychic pain. While en route to Greenhaven, they are confronted by the Archfey Silvertail. She interrogates the party and demonstrates that she could easily annihilate them if they were to interfere with her pets again. Silvertail learns the names of Bhardrus and Astor Mephisto, but is unable to obtain the full names of the remaining members of the party.

In Greenhaven, Bhardrus and Jozeth attend a monk tournament at the Crimson Monastery, while the rest of the party splits up to gather information around the city. During their investigations, they learn of a strange illness that is killing the rumored illegitimate members of a prominent vampire family. They are also approached for a security detail job by an individual posing as a noble, who claims his wife’s necklace has gone missing and needs to be recovered. Additionally, the party visits the Thieves’ Guild in search of information, but instead becomes entangled in a smuggling operation. They are coerced into transporting drugs to Seluris and delivering them to the Bladesong family.

During the tournament, Jozeth and Bhardrus encounter Malakai Kane, who clearly harbors a grudge against Jozeth. Malakai withdraws during the finals and later requests a meeting with Jozeth and his companions on the outskirts of the city the following day, intending to fight them. The confrontation occurs as planned, and after acknowledging the party’s strength, Malakai rewards them with soul coins - magical items capable of triggering a long rest sequence when flipped, even in the midst of combat. He also warns Jozeth that they will face each other again once Jozeth returns to his old home. Later that same day, the party meets with the king, who reveals that there was a rumour of 7 artifacts that hold the power to destroy or create. The Triumvirate were only able to find 1 artifact each and is safeguarded in their respective islands to prevent misuse. The party were tasked with locating all of the artifacts and either securing them or returning them to the Triumvirate for protection.

During their stay in Greenhaven, the party visits Sophie Dewborn, a member of a vampire bloodline. She reveals that the current heir of the family may be eliminating illegitimate branches by draining their blood, while the general populace believes the deaths to be the result of a spreading disease. She reveals that the Wizard Tower is looking into this disease, not aware that it could be the handiwork of a vampire. The party makes contact with the Wizard Tower, where they learn of Damien Hart, a mage researching the strange illness. His findings indicate that the victims, having been drained of blood, exhibit rapid physical deterioration, with their bodies crumbling and withering far faster than normal. He also mentions that populace is scared of it being disease but it very much looks like a curse that is being disguised.

Seeking further answers, the party sneaks into the residence of Lucinda Duskborn after an earlier failed attempt to speak with her directly and resolve things peacefully. Inside, they discover that she has been conducting experiments on living humans with vampire ancestry, attempting to turn them into full vampires rather than dhampirs. The party confronts and defeats Lucinda, recovering a set of cursed gauntlets, an ancient book and her diary. The diary reveals that Lucinda pledged her allegiance to a powerful man, as he was able to turn her into a vampire with only magic and was instructed to create a cadre of vampires to help increase her influence and power. Shortly thereafter, the party moves to complete the quest of finding the lost necklace. During this quest, they learn that the noble who hired them to retrieve the necklace was, in fact, another member of the Cult, sent to retrieve the Mirror - one of the Artifacts - from them. Although the PCs survive the encounter, the extensive damage caused in the area draws unwanted attention, leaving them marked as persons of interest.

When the party visits the Wizard Tower to arrange travel to the next island and speak with Damien, they encounter his mentor, Zarkon Sonier. Zarkon invites them into his office and requests Lucinda’s diary, which the party hands over. He then politely asks them to surrender the Mirror they possess. A difficult battle ensues, but the PCs manage to defeat the powerful archmage - only to discover that the Zarkon they killed was merely a clone, a simulacrum. Following the confrontation, the party escapes the island and successfully makes their way to Seluris, as a bounty is placed on their head for the attempted murder of the Archmage Zarkon.

Upon reaching Seluris, the party immediately travels north to the Avloria Grove, which was also the birth place of Varis. From the outside, the grove appears lush and vibrant, giving the impression of good health. However, as they venture deeper inside, they discover that the land is slowly decaying into a swamp. In the middle of the night, the party makes its way to the World Tree, where they encounter a green hag. The hag does not attack them outright and instead attempts to evade confrontation. As the party continues to press her, she flees, leading them to her coven’s dwelling, where she and a night hag engage the party in combat. The night hag escapes once it becomes clear the battle cannot be won, while the green hag is knocked unconscious.

The party withdraws and returns to the World Tree, which they find to be diseased and dying. The following morning, they meet with Varis’s family and observe that they treat him not as a loved one, but as someone burdened with responsibility for their well-being. His sister was dying to the infection of the World Tree and he wasn't sure how he could save her so had left to visit Azeroth. Varis later visits his master’s room and finds it empty, save for enchanted writing on the wall. The message, left by Oakenshade and written specifically for Varis, remains unreadable to him, though it serves as a warning about the Ancient Order gathering on Stormwreck Island. After spending the day in the grove, the party begins traveling toward the main city of Thelidorei. Along the way, they encounter the hags once more and kill them, burying their bodies afterward. Upon the hags’ deaths, the surrounding grove brightens slightly, suggesting a partial cleansing of the corruption. The party also learns that the hags were members of the Hourglass Coven, led by three sisters.

Continuing their journey to Thelidorei, the party is ambushed by cultists. The skirmish was planned to fail and was given another objective in which they succeeded. Upon entering Thelidorei, the party establishes themselves at a nearby tavern and takes on several minor quests to earn gold and build a reputation, eventually gaining enough recognition to access the Noble District.

Jozeth returns to his former home, where he finds Malakai waiting for him, prepared for another confrontation. This time, however, the party manages to de-escalate the situation. Malakai reveals that the current Joestar family was responsible for the murder of his family, and that his original intention was to wipe out every member of the Joestar bloodline in retaliation. Through extended discussion, the party convinces him that his vengeance should be directed only at those truly responsible. In exchange for the promise that he would be allowed to kill Dario Joestar, Malakai agrees to abandon his plans of indiscriminate revenge. Before parting, he also reveals that Erina Joestar is still alive and being held captive at the estate of Zeish Joestar, Jozeth’s uncle.

The party then approaches the remaining four noble houses, seeking their approval to bring an issue before the Council of Stars, the ruling body of Thelidorei. Each house demands a favor in exchange for its support. **House Aurelian** tasks the party with reclaiming a ship bound for Ortan Thaig that is transporting silk and secretly being used to smuggle drugs. **House Eldran** asks them to repair a ruptured leyline that is causing dangerous elemental disturbances. **House Vaelora** offers its approval without demanding any service, requesting only that the party leave them undisturbed. **House Miraval** requires evidence proving that the Bladesong family is involved in illegal activities. The evidence against the Bladesong family ultimately leads to their estate being burnt down, as they are exposed as unwilling participants forced to assist House Aurelian in drug smuggling operations.

House Joestar is the final house the party had to confront. To earn its approval, Jozeth was forced into a duel with Zeish Joestar, who resorts to underhanded tactics once Jozeth begins to gain the upper hand. After several rounds of combat, Malakai arrives unexpectedly. He kills Dario Joestar and then Zeish, honoring his promise to Jozeth. Having fulfilled his vengeance, Malakai spares the remaining members of the Joestar family, pledges his support to Jozeth, and departs. Erina Joestar is freed from captivity and named the new head of House Joestar. The house is converted into a bastion and library for Jozeth Joestar’s use, and the party is given a letter granting them access to a ship for future travel.

With sufficient noble support secured, the party proceeds to the Council of the Stars. Upon arrival, they meet Therrin Thornwarden and Faelywn Oakenshade. Therrin displays open prejudice toward the rest of the party and speaks only to Varis, while Faelywn entrusts sensitive information solely to Varis, warning him of the Ancient Order - powerful dragons gathering on Stormwreck Island that would lead an army. Varis shares the deteriorating state of Avloria Grove and voices his suspicion that the hags are responsible for its corruption. As a result of their findings, the council meeting is postponed, and the party returns to the grove to confront the hags they believe are causing its decay.

The party returns to the forest and kills two of the hag sisters. During the aftermath, a wizard appears and provides the party with potions of healing, grateful that they spared his brother, who had previously fought alongside the hags against them. The third sister escapes, as she can only be slain during a solar eclipse. With the immediate threat reduced, the party returns to Thelidorei to attend the council meeting. Before the council convenes, the party seeks out Therrin once more to secure his support. Varis agrees to assist him with a personal matter in exchange for that backing. Faelywn Oakenshade is nowhere to be found, having already departed for Stormwreck Island to investigate the gathering of the Ancient Order. 

The party also meets Queen Liri, an Archfey rumored to be under the influence of the Hourglass Coven. The conversation proves unexpectedly cordial, and the PCs succeed in earning her support. Pressed for time, the party heads toward the council chambers, but along the way they encounter Aelor Starbloom. Aelor convinces Bhardrus that he is the king’s illegitimate son and reveals that he was imprisoned by Sylwen Duskwhisper. Meanwhile, Sylwen intercepts the rest of the party en route to the meeting and takes a particular interest in Jozeth, especially as he attempts to charm her. While Sylwen is distracted, Bhardrus attempts to free Aelor from his cell but is discovered by the guards. The rest of the party soon joins him, defeats the guards, and secures Aelor’s escape. They promise to bring him safely before the council.

The Council of Stars is composed of representatives from each of Seluris’s dominant powers. These include the delegates of Thelidorei - Therrin Thornwarden and Faelywn Oakenshade (absent); the Fey representative, Queen Liri of the Whispered Pact Coven; and the representatives of the nobility - King Aerion Starbloom, Lady Sylwen Duskwhisper, and Galadriel Vaelora. The council addresses several major issues. The proposed removal of Seluris from the Triumvirate is denied, as is the motion to remove Therrin Thornwarden from the council for his actions. Assistance for Avloria Grove is approved. Finally, the council passes judgment on Tschisy Kragon for theft from the royal house. Tschisy is pardoned after offering his aid in recovering the artifact, and the party is tasked with keeping watch over him.

After the meeting concludes, Aelor Starbloom reveals himself publicly. A heated argument erupts between Aelor and Sylwen, after which King Aerion declares that Aelor will succeed him as king, with Sylwen remaining as regent while Aelor undergoes his training. Aelor promises the party his support whenever it is needed. Later that evening, Sylwen approaches Jozeth privately and asks whether he would support her if she were to wage war against Aelor. Through careful persuasion, Jozeth convinces her to abandon the idea, successfully averting a potential civil war in Seluris. That night, Varis continues to write in a letter which Tschisy and Astor notice; Astor attempts to read it and is injured by its magic, while Tschisy simply steals it.

The following day, the party is summoned to a meeting with King Aerion. He reveals that the stolen item is one of the seven Artifacts and tasks the party with locating the remaining ones. He also asks them to perform a personal favor: spying on Galadriel Vaelora, who is rumored to be involved in suspicious activities. During this investigation, the party discovers that Galadriel is being controlled by an aboleth, which is assisting her in creating an undead army to seize control of Thelidorei. During the mission, a doll hidden within one of the rooms animates, burrows into Varis, and embeds itself within his body and soul.
