The Material Plane, known as **Valtorra**, was once composed of five great continents: **Thiranys**, **Myrraline**, **Uln’Dakar**, **Aeltherion**, and **Elaria**. Separated by vast oceans, each landmass bore witness to the shaping of the world during the primordial age.

In the beginning, there was only **Annam, the All-Father**, who shaped Valtorra from the raw void. From his will came the first beings — **Giants** and **Dragons** — the only creatures who could not truly die, only grow older and more powerful. These titanic beings walked the world in an age before time, their clashes so devastating they reshaped mountains, split seas, and carved canyons into the earth itself.

Legends speak of a great war between the Giants and the Dragons — a war whose end remains disputed. Some say the Giants were victorious, having slain an **Aspect of Tiamat** in battle. Others claim it was the Dragons who prevailed, driving **Annam** himself from the universe in a display of their might.

With the All-Father gone, the **fledgling gods** emerged from the cosmic remnants over taking the Old Gods. They carved dominions for themselves across the five continents and brought forth **life**: elves, dwarves, humans, and countless other mortal races. These younger peoples spread across the land, settling where they could survive, worshipping the gods, and slowly building civilizations and cultures.

Of the five continents, **Thiranys** was the first to cast aside the old ways. A land forged by intellect and fire, it became a cradle for inventors, philosophers, and alchemists — a society where **only the strength of the mind** commanded respect. Sorcery without logic, and might without purpose, held little value unless they fueled **progress**.

Over generations, the people of Thiranys **turned from the gods**, rejecting divine influence as superstition and weakness. No temples remain in its cities — only **smokestacks, titanic forges, and towers of glass and brass** that pierce the sky that help to invent machines of war. The worship of any deity is forbidden, and even speaking of divine miracles invites suspicion, exile, or worse. In Thiranys, **faith lies in invention**, and salvation is found not in prayer, but in steel and steam.

Where Thiranys scorched the sky with smoke and steel, **Myrraline** remains cloaked in eternal green — a continent swallowed by ancient forests and shrouded in **primordial mystery**. While the fledgling gods carved their thrones across the other lands, the people of Myrraline turned instead to the **Old Gods of Nature**, forgotten by most, but still pulsing with raw, untamed power.

Over the ages, the forests deepened and grew wilder, sheltering sacred places untouched by mortal hands. Only the **most blessed of druids** are permitted to walk the inner woods, where reality thins and the trees are said to whisper with the voices of the divine. At the heart of it all lies a **fragment of the World Tree**, a relic of creation fiercely guarded by the **Concords of Beasts** — sentient creatures of immense age and wisdom, bound to the Old Gods.

Civilization in Myrraline survives only at the fringes, where villages cling to the forest's edge like lichen on bark. The deeper one goes, the less the land belongs to mortals. Some say **Thal'Zirra**, the Plane of the Old Gods and wild magic, bleeds into this continent, warping time and space — and drawing in the faithful and the foolish alike.

Towards the north of the Plane, **Uln’Dakar** is whispered of more than it is known — a **land of fire, stone, and legend**, believed to be the birthplace of the **first Dragons**. Here, the **Primordial Dragons** — ancient beings who once warred with the True Giants and battled **Annam, the All-Father** himself — are said to slumber beneath ever-churning volcanoes, their dreams shaping the land above.

The surface of Uln’Dakar is a **searing wasteland**, scorched by unrelenting heat and riddled with rivers of molten rock. Long ago, the mortal civilizations that once thrived here **fled beneath the earth**, carving homes deep within obsidian caverns and subterranean chasms. Only the hardiest remain above, dwelling in cities forged from black glass and **shielded by enchanted domes**, constantly battling the wrath of a land alive with flame.

Few venture to Uln’Dakar without cause — and fewer still return. Those who do speak of **volcanoes that breathe**, **storms of cinders that whisper in Draconic**, and ancient ruins where the very air hums with raw, reality-bending power. Whether the **Primordial Dragons** truly remain in the heart of the mountains or exist only in myth, none can say — but the land itself seems to remember.

Far to the south lies **Aeltherion**, a **frozen continent sculpted from living crystal and ancient, enchanted ice**. Shrouded in frost and silence, it is whispered to be the **birthplace of the True Giants**, formed from the breath of Annam himself in the dawn of time.

The land is dominated by **colossal formations** — which legends say are not mere rocks, but **slumbering giants**, beings so immense and old they became one with the land. Their breath shapes the blizzards, and their dreams ripple through the auroras that dance across the night sky.

Only a handful of **mortal tribes** endure the continent's cold embrace, surviving through **ancient pacts and rituals** shared by the Frost Giants who still roam the tundra. These mortals do not worship the gods, but instead **commune with the land itself** — listening to the crystal’s hum, the wind’s song, and the deep pulses of the world’s slumbering titans.

Of all the continents of Valtorra, **Elaria** is the most **veiled in mystery**. Once a beacon of harmony and wonder, it was said to be a **sanctuary of balance**, where both the **Old Gods of Nature** and the **fledgling Gods of the Divine** were welcomed as stewards, not rulers. It was a land of **breathless beauty**, where vast forests, golden plains, and crystalline coasts stretched unbroken from sea to sea. Freedom was more than a belief — it was **woven into the soul of the land**.

But nearly a thousand years ago, that harmony was torn apart in a cataclysm known only as **The Shattering** — an event so violent, so absolute, that it **broke the continent into seven sundered isles**. Countless lives were lost, cities were ground to dust, and the heart of Elaria’s wisdom was shattered alongside its stone. What caused it remains unknown. Some blame the wrath of the fledgling Gods or the wrath of beings of power that fell from grace, known as the Dark Lords.

But nothing stays broken forever — life remembered how to grow. Brave souls resettled the **Shattered Isles**, clinging to fragments of the past. Yet the cultures that rose were **echoes** — dim reflections of what came before. What little knowledge survived became **songs, riddles, and half-remembered myths**, passed down by bards and gleemen. Even though a millennia has passed, the people live in fear and caution as strange events have taken place in these isles. Winters that grew **unnaturally harsh** on one isle, while an eternal drought chokes another.
